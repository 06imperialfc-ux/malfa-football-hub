Privacy and data protection

POPIA notice

How MALFA handles personal information on this competition platform.

Responsible party

The Mamelodi Local Football Association (MALFA) is the responsible party for personal information processed through this website. Privacy enquiries and requests may be sent to info@malfa.co.za. MALFA must confirm this as its official Information Officer contact before final launch.

Information processed

The public platform mainly contains competition information, including clubs, crests, fixtures, results, tables, news and partners. Personal information may also include administrator email addresses and authentication records, correspondence sent to MALFA, images or names included in authorised news content, and technical records such as IP addresses and device information generated by hosting and security services.

Purposes

Information is processed to administer MALFA competitions, publish accurate football information, manage authorised access, secure and maintain the platform, communicate with clubs and the public, keep appropriate sporting records, and comply with legal obligations.

Children and junior football

MALFA will not publish a child’s personal profile, identifying photograph or other personal information unless a valid lawful basis exists and, where required, prior consent has been obtained from a competent person. Identity numbers, medical information, home addresses, school details and private contact details must not be entered into this website.

Operators and international processing

MALFA uses Supabase for database hosting, authentication and media storage, and Vercel for website hosting and delivery. These providers may process information outside South Africa. MALFA will maintain appropriate written operator arrangements and transfer safeguards for such processing.

Security

MALFA uses access controls, row-level database rules, encrypted connections, restricted administrator accounts and security monitoring. No system can be guaranteed completely secure. Suspected security compromises will be investigated and reported as required by law.

Retention

Personal information is kept only for as long as reasonably necessary for the stated purpose, a legal requirement, dispute handling or an authorised sporting archive. Administrator access is removed when no longer required. Images and identifying content concerning children must be reviewed regularly and removed when the purpose or authorisation ends.

Your rights

A data subject may ask whether MALFA holds personal information about them and may request access, correction, deletion, restriction or objection where the law permits. Requests should identify the relevant person and record clearly. MALFA may need to verify identity before acting on a request.

Complaints

Questions should first be sent to MALFA’s Information Officer. A person may also lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator of South Africa.

Local storage and authentication

The public site stores a theme preference on the visitor’s device. The administrator dashboard uses Supabase authentication storage to maintain an authorised sign-in session. The platform does not currently use advertising or behavioural-tracking cookies.

Updates

This notice may be updated when the platform, providers or processing activities change. Last updated: 17 July 2026.
